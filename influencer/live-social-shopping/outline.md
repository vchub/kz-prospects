# Live Social Shopping - Idea Outline

## 1) Objective
Define the platform idea, promising features, proven global patterns, and current trends for live social shopping.

## 2) Product thesis
If we combine creator trust + live interaction + low-friction checkout, we can improve conversion compared to static influencer placements.

## 3) Core product building blocks
1. Live session infrastructure
- Scheduled and drop-in live streams
- Product pinning during live
- Co-host mode (creator + brand rep)

2. Commerce layer
- In-stream product cards
- Minimal-click checkout flow
- Cart and order confirmation linked to live session

3. Social engagement layer
- Real-time chat/moderation
- Live polls and audience prompts
- Urgency mechanics (countdown, limited inventory, live-only offers)

4. Performance and attribution layer
- Session-level metrics: viewers, engaged viewers, clicks, add-to-cart, purchases
- Host/product level conversion reporting
- Hybrid attribution support for partial-tracking channels

5. Creator and brand workflow
- Standard live campaign briefs
- Approval and content/compliance checklist
- Rights usage terms for replay and paid amplification

6. Monetization and payout
- Hybrid payouts: fixed live fee + performance component
- Transparent creator earnings dashboard
- Fast payout cycle and dispute flow

## 4) Most promising features (priority shortlist)
1. Live commerce campaign templates by vertical (beauty, electronics, grocery, fashion)
2. Host performance scorecard (conversion quality, retention, refund profile)
3. Real-time conversion dashboard for brands during stream
4. Replay monetization (post-live shoppable playback)
5. Promo-code + link + in-platform event attribution in one model
6. Rights and whitelisting controls for high-performing live clips
7. AI-assisted live script prompts and offer timing suggestions
8. Fraud/risk alerts (suspicious traffic, abnormal conversion spikes)

## 5) What has worked globally
- Integrated flows where discovery, live content, transaction, and post-purchase ops are connected.
- Strong host-led trust loops (demo + Q&A + objection handling).
- Repeated formats (weekly shows, category drops) instead of one-off campaigns.
- Blended monetization models for creators, not pure commission only.

## 6) What has failed or underperformed
- Standalone feature experiments without deep commerce integration.
- Incentive-heavy growth with weak long-term unit economics.
- Poor logistics/returns experience that breaks repeat purchase behavior.
- Weak measurement transparency for brands and creators.

## 7) Trend signals to monitor (2025-2026)
- Shift from vanity metrics to commerce-verified outcomes.
- Consolidation of creator tools into end-to-end partnership platforms.
- AI moving into daily workflow (briefing, scripting, optimization).
- Greater emphasis on compliance, disclosure, and ad transparency.
- Growth of live + short video + affiliate hybrid models.

## 8) Key risks for this idea
- Platform dependency risk (API/policy changes on major social channels).
- Attribution gaps outside owned checkout surfaces.
- Creator quality variance and moderation complexity during live sessions.
- Fulfillment and return friction affecting repeat conversion.

## 9) Subproject output target
A concept-validated feature set for Live Social Shopping that can be compared against the broader influencer platform roadmap.
