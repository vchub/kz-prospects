# Influencer Platform Outline (Idea + Features + Global Signals)

## Goal of this document
Define the platform concept and shortlist the most promising features, based on:
- what has already worked globally,
- what is trending now,
- what is realistic for our market.

Not an actionable execution plan yet.

## Product scope (updated assumptions)
- We do **not** build influencer search/discovery as a core feature now.
- We start with an initial creator pool and grow through referrals/word of mouth.
- The platform value should come from workflow, monetization, trust, and measurable outcomes.

## Channels to support (beyond YouTube + Telegram)
Primary:
- TikTok
- Instagram (Reels/Stories/Posts)
- YouTube (Long-form/Shorts/Live)
- Telegram channels

Secondary:
- Facebook
- Twitch
- X (Twitter)
- Snapchat
- Brand e-commerce pages / landing pages
- Marketplaces where affiliate/product-tagging is possible (e.g., WB/Wibes-like flows)

## Core platform idea
A creator-commerce operating system for micro and mid-tier creators:
- Brands launch structured offers.
- Creators accept and deliver content.
- Platform handles deliverables, attribution options, payouts, and compliance.
- Performance is measured with channel-appropriate metrics (CPA when possible, CPV/CPC/hybrid when not).

## Most promising features to have (priority list)
1. Offer Marketplace + Standardized Briefs
- Brands publish offers with fixed terms, expected deliverables, payout model, timing.
- Creators accept in one click (no long DM negotiation loop).
- Why promising: global platforms win by reducing campaign setup friction.

2. Hybrid Compensation Engine (Fixed + Performance)
- Supports: fixed fee + CPA/CPS, fixed fee + CPC, fixed fee + CPV.
- Why promising: reduces creator risk while preserving performance incentives for brands.

3. Multi-channel Attribution Layer
- Track links where possible.
- Add promo-code attribution where links are weak.
- Add pre-lander click tracking for closed ecosystems.
- Why promising: mixed attribution is the practical standard in fragmented channel ecosystems.

4. Contracting + Content Approvals + Rights Management
- Digital agreements, deliverable acceptance, revision workflow.
- Usage rights/licensing terms for brand reuse and paid amplification.
- Why promising: mature platforms treat legal and content rights as core, not optional.

5. Fast Payouts + Tax/Compliance Layer
- Wallet, pending/available balances, payout status.
- Tax withholding/reporting support where needed.
- Why promising: payment speed and compliance confidence strongly affect creator retention.

6. Creative Copilot (AI)
- Brief-to-script drafts by channel format.
- Local-language tone variants.
- CTA and hook suggestions by category.
- Why promising: AI-assisted creator workflows are now standard direction across major ecosystems.

7. Brand-side Measurement Dashboard
- Unified campaign view by creator/channel/product.
- KPI split by funnel stage (awareness, traffic, sales intent, sales).
- Fraud/anomaly flags.
- Why promising: buyers increasingly demand standardization and ROI clarity.

8. UGC Licensing + Paid Amplification Workflow
- Brand can whitelist/boost high-performing creator content.
- Rights and spend linkage in one flow.
- Why promising: global trend is merging influencer campaigns with paid media amplification.

## What has been successful before (global patterns)
- Closed-loop shopping ecosystems (creator content + product tagging + attribution + payout in one system).
- Platform-managed campaign workflow (briefing -> approvals -> reporting -> payment).
- Combining organic creator content with paid amplification.
- Building creator trust via predictable payouts and transparent stats.
- Performance models adapted by channel reality (not forcing CPA where it cannot be measured reliably).

## What tends to fail or underperform
- Pure CPA-only model for smaller creators in markets with weak attribution.
- Over-reliance on one platform/API policy.
- Weak payment reliability (delays, opaque calculations).
- No clear rights/licensing model for using creator content in paid ads.
- Unit economics based on heavy subsidies without durable value.

## Trends to pay attention to now (2025-2026)
- Creator spend keeps growing and is treated as a core media channel.
- Stronger demand for standardized measurement and creator-brand matching quality.
- AI is moving from experimentation to day-to-day campaign workflow.
- Commerce-enabled content (tagged products, affiliate flows, in-platform shopping surfaces) is expanding.
- Blended influencer + performance media operating model is becoming default.

## Candidate feature set for v1 (idea-level shortlist)
Must-have:
- Offer marketplace
- Hybrid compensation setup
- Mixed attribution (link + promo code + pre-lander)
- Contract/approval workflow
- Wallet + payout + compliance basics
- Unified reporting for brands and creators

Nice-to-have (v1.5+):
- AI script assistant
- Advanced anti-fraud scoring
- Content rights marketplace and automated licensing tiers
- Deeper paid media activation tools
