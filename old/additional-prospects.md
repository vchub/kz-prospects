Emerging AdTech and Retail Innovation Opportunities for Kazakhstan (2025-2026): A Strategic Report on Digital Infrastructure, Sovereign AI, and Automated CommerceThe retail and advertising landscape in Kazakhstan is currently defined by a paradox of rapid digital acceleration and structural physical constraints. As of the first half of 2025, the retail e-commerce market reached KZT 1.7 trillion, marking a 19% year-on-year increase in local currency terms. This growth is occurring against a backdrop of a deepening labor crisis, where an estimated 3 million skilled workers will be required over the next three to five years to sustain national development. The central tension in the market lies between the overwhelming dominance of the Kaspi.kz ecosystem—which accounts for approximately 70% of e-commerce value—and the urgent need for independent "middleware" solutions that allow the broader retail sector to achieve operational efficiency and data sovereignty.The Macro-Economic Trajectory of Kazakhstani E-CommerceThe maturation of Kazakhstan’s digital economy is evidenced by the shifting relationship between transaction volume and order value. While the number of transactions surged by 27% in the first half of 2025, the average order value (AOV) declined by 6% in tenge and 18% in US dollars. As of late 2025, employers posted nearly 110,000 job vacancies, yet only 71,500 resumes were submitted, leaving a gap of 38,000 unfilled positions. By acting as a central hub, it provides real-time inventory synchronization across multiple sales channels, including international marketplaces like Trendyol and Amazon.
